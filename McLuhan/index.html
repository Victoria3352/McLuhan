“The Medium is the Massage”: Reflection

The immediate anti-establishmentarian theme presented in “The Medium is the Massage” is a

real throwback to the 1960’s counterculture movements that espoused similar messages with

images and calls for radical change, shedding the real in search of the altruistic. Ironically, as a

speaking point expressed throughout the reading is the “rear view mirror society” that currently

clings to the old ways of physicality and mechanization as opposed to transgressing into the

electrical revolution that has no structure, limits, or goals. Now just one big tribe is being formed

worldwide with a unified purpose, the jungle drums beat to the rhythm of revolution, drastic

reconstructions, and freedom from all that constricted our previous generations! Tune in drop out

or whatever they professed back in the day, but does this really apply now? How has the

electronic age, the cyber world of today changed the old guard? Has the flood of information and

data at our fingertips influenced our lives to the point that we shed our skins like a snake that has

outgrown the past? The alphabet and the written word are repeatedly mentioned in the article

and, most specifically the specialism and fomenting and fostering of segmentation detachment

and the fragmenting process that resulted whereas, the electric technology is purported to foster

and encourage unification and involvement yet, much of what I see today is complete

detachment from the reality of life and a withdrawal by many in society into their own electric

world. Even more pronounced are the decisive stratum that have formed and the widening gap in

inter-socialization among the varied groups in our culture with deeper hatred and discrimination,

senseless violence and racism that is fueled by those using the electric medium with little to no

consequences or accountability. Perhaps the musings found in this article are meant to be taken

as sarcasm or tongue in cheek but the truth of the matter is we are not living in the matrix, the

real world must exist, the electric world as the author describes is really only one switch away

from going dark and then this subculture really will be returning to their primordial selves!
